# 📊 Analytical Methodology


Following discussions with stakeholders, several business concerns were raised regarding commercial performance, customer contribution, and product-level sales dynamics. While transaction data was available, the main challenge was to bridge the gap between business needs and analytical approaches.

---
#### Problem Framing

The first step consisted of clarifying stakeholder expectations and translating them into measurable analytical questions.

Instead of broad requests such as *“understand performance better”* or *“identify growth opportunities”*, the analysis was structured around:

- Revenue evolution over time  
- Product and category contribution  
- Customer value and behavior  
- Sales concentration and dependency  

#### Data Understanding

Once the analytical scope was defined, the dataset was reviewed in order to:

- Identify relevant variables  
- Assess data completeness and usability  
- Ensure alignment with business questions  

This step ensured that the analysis was feasible and grounded in available data.

#### Data Preparation

A data preparation phase was conducted to ensure reliability of results:

- Validation of data formats  
- Handling missing or inconsistent values  
- Creation of derived metrics required for analysis  

#### Analytical Approach

The analysis followed a progressive structure:

- Global performance overview using key KPIs  
- Time-based analysis to understand trends  
- Product and category analysis to identify key contributors  
- Customer analysis to evaluate value and behavior  

This approach allowed a shift from a high-level view to more granular insights.

---

##  Key Takeaways from the Stakeholder Meeting

#### Sales Team
- Revenue fluctuations are not fully explained  
- Need to understand whether growth comes from volume, basket size, or specific categories  
- Current reporting lacks explanatory depth  

#### Category / Product Team
- Strong differences in category performance  
- Possible revenue concentration in a small number of products  
- Need visibility on value-driving products  

#### Marketing / CRM
- Customer lifetime value is unclear  
- Uncertainty about reliance on high-value customers vs broad base  
- Need segmentation between repeat and one-time buyers  

#### Operations / Logistics
- Delivery issues may impact customer satisfaction  
- Interest in linking delivery delays to review scores  
- Lack of unified view across order journey and feedback  

#### Leadership
- Data exists but is not easily translated into decisions  
- Need for consolidated, actionable insights  
- Expectation of business-oriented analysis  

#### Finance / Performance Monitoring
- KPI consistency across datasets is critical  
- Risk of duplicated records when joining multiple sources  
- Need to define analysis grain before dashboarding  

---

#### Business Questions

Stakeholder needs were translated into analytical questions:

- How does sales performance evolve over time?  
- Which products and categories contribute most to revenue?  
- Which customers generate the most value?  
- Is revenue concentrated among a small group of customers or products?  
- What purchasing patterns can inform business decisions?  
- Which insights can be translated into actionable recommendations?  

---

#### Outcome

This methodology provides the foundation for a structured, data-driven analysis aimed at transforming raw business concerns into actionable insights supporting decision-making.
