## Analytical Methodology

Following a meeting with stakeholders, several business concerns were raised regarding commercial performance, customer contribution, and product-level sales dynamics. While transaction data was available, the main challenge was to bridge the gap between business needs and analytical approaches available.

As data analysts, the first step was therefore to clarify stakeholder expectations and translate them into measurable analytical questions. Rather than working directly from raw requests such as “understand performance better” or “identify growth opportunities”. Broad concerns related to performance were reformulated into measurable questions around revenue evolution, category and product contribution, customer value, and sales concentration.

Once the analytical scope was defined, the dataset was reviewed to identify the variables required to address these questions and to assess the overall usability of the data. This step ensured that the analysis remained aligned with both stakeholder expectations and data availability.

A data preparation phase was then carried out in order to secure the reliability of the results. This included validating formats, checking for missing or inconsistent values, and creating derived indicators necessary for the analysis.

The analytical work then followed a progressive structure. It began with a global performance diagnosis through key indicators, before moving into deeper analysis across time, products, categories, and customers. This progression made it possible to move from an overall business view to a more detailed understanding of the factors driving performance.

Finally, the results were consolidated into business insights and recommendations designed to answer the initial stakeholder concerns and support future commercial decisions.

### Key takeaways from the stakeholder meeting
we list the stakeholder meeting notes : 
**Sales team**
-Revenue changes from one period to another, but the reasons are not always clear.
-There is a need to understand whether performance is driven by more orders, higher basket size, or a few strong categories.
-Current reporting shows the top-line numbers, but not enough explanation behind them.
**Category / product team**
-Some categories seem to perform much better than others, but this has not been clearly measured yet.
-There is a feeling that a small number of products may account for a large share of sales.
-The team would like more visibility on which products really drive business value, not just volume.
**Marketing / CRM**
-Customer acquisition is happening, but customer value over time is not well understood.
-It is unclear whether revenue comes from a broad customer base or from a limited group of high-value customers.
-The team would like a better view of repeat customers versus one-time buyers.
**Operations / logistics**
-Delivery issues come up regularly and may be affecting customer experience.
-There is interest in checking whether late deliveries or long delivery times are linked to lower review scores.
-The team does not yet have a consolidated view between order journey and customer feedback.
**Leadership**
-A lot of data is available, but it is still difficult to turn it into clear business priorities.
-There is a need for a more consolidated and decision-oriented view of performance.
-Leadership is expecting insights that can lead to concrete business actions, not just descriptive reporting.
**Finance / performance monitoring**
-KPI consistency is important, especially because the analysis will rely on several datasets.
-There is concern that joining multiple tables too quickly may create duplicated records or misleading indicators.
-Before building dashboards, the data structure and the level of analysis need to be clarified.

### Translation of Stakeholder Needs into Analytical Questions

Following stakeholder discussions, the analysis was structured by translating broad business expectations into measurable questions.

- A need to better understand overall commercial performance became: How does sales performance evolve over time?
- A need to identify the main drivers of revenue became: Which products and categories contribute most to revenue?
- A need to understand customer contribution became: Which customers generate the most value?
- A need to assess dependency and concentration risk became: Is performance concentrated among a limited share of customers or products?
- A need to better understand purchasing dynamics became: What patterns in purchasing behavior could inform business action?
- A need to support decision-making became: Which findings can be translated into actionable recommendations?
